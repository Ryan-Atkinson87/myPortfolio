I’m Ryan — a software engineer who can’t really leave a problem alone once I’ve noticed it. Give me something tedious, fragile or closed-off, and I’ll quietly start building the version I wish existed.

That instinct is how most of my work begins. I keep tropical fish, so I built an open-source monitor for them. I ride indoors on Zwift, so I built a workout editor the existing tools never bothered to. My son asked for a robot we could actually control, so we built Robert together. The throughline is the same: find the real problem, build it properly, then keep going until it’s something other people can use too.

Underneath all of it sits Trive Dev — my own venture and the umbrella these projects ship under. It’s where I get to own the whole thing: the architecture, the design system, the infrastructure, and increasingly the AI workflows that help me move faster without cutting corners.

I care about craft, but I care more about shipping. Auth, accessibility, error tracking, real deployments — the unglamorous parts that decide whether something is a demo or a product. I’m looking for work where that end-to-end ownership is valued, whether that’s a team to grow with or clients who need someone who can carry an idea all the way to live.
