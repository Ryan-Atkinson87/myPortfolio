Zwift workout files are plain XML. People who build structured training plans end up repeating the same warm-up and cool-down blocks across dozens of files, and changing them means editing each one by hand, tedious and error-prone.

The two main competitors offer visual editors but are dated and have no concept of reusable blocks or bulk editing across workouts. Zwift Tool fills that gap: a clean, modern editor with a three-section model, a personal block library, and a bulk-replace that can swap a section across many saved workouts in one action and export the lot as a zip.

It is a genuinely useful community tool, and a demonstration of full-stack product thinking, shipped from a side project with real users.
