I keep tropical fish, and monitoring water conditions by hand is tedious and easy to neglect. Commercial monitors exist but they’re expensive, locked to proprietary ecosystems, and offer no visibility into how they work.

Aquasense started as a personal fix: continuously track temperature, flow, humidity and water level across multiple tanks, publish to a live dashboard, and alert me when something’s wrong — while understanding every part of it, from the wiring up to the software architecture.

It grew from a breadboard prototype into a structured Python agent running as a systemd service, with a custom PCB HAT in design and a 3D-printed enclosure. It is fully open source — the maker and aquarium communities both lack good, documented DIY monitoring at this level.
